Kids Buddy: Application for Slow Learning Children with flexible learning methods

Your Child's Learning Supporter.
Application in Sinhala for slow learning children whose age in 4-6 in Sri Lanka.

Let's begin

Our Story

A little bit about Kids Buddy

In the learning process of a child, they pass through different stages. Every child is an individual and does not posses the same abilities when learning. It is essential to provide primary education and proper guidance to a child at early childhood. According to the interviews conducted with doctors, teachers and parents, it was revealed that slow learning children lack basic skills when compared to a normal child.

The application is comprised with four features namely object recognizer, communication trainer, pronunciation trainer and math trainer. Tools and technologies like tensorflow, opencv, Deep learning, Machine learning and Image Processing are used to develop these features. This application will benefit slow learning children immensely to improve their communication skills, objection identification, pronunciation and mathematical skills. Overall system will help the slow learning child to improve their basic education.

Main Objective and Sub Objectives

Research Main Objective Developing an android application to improve slow learning children's skills of age 4-6

Click and Find Me Improving visual skills of slow learners by analysing how these children identify objects and helping them to differentiate objects

Talk with Dolly Improving Sinhala communication skills of slow learners by using an AI chatbot

Say it Right Improving Sinhla pronuncition os slow learners by analysing how slow learners pronounce Sinhala letters, words and phrases

Math Champ Improving basic mathematical skills of slow learners by using games and time-based tests

Research Project Scope

Literature Survey

Sri Lanka is a country that has two main native languages.They are Sinhala and Tamil. 75% of the population in Sri Lanka use Sinhala as their native language and most of the children in Sri Lanka use Sinhala language since their birth. In human brain development process, at the birth of a child, the child’s brain has already grown to 25% of the adult size and at the age of 3 the brain reaches 80% of its adult size.While children pass those two stages, curiosity and desire to learn about the world increases. Even though a child has the curiosity and desire to learn new things, every child is not very good at their learning process. Some children’s learning ability is not at an average level, but less than it. Those children are categorized as slow learners.These children lack basic skills like communication skills, grammatical skills, pronunciation, object recognition skills.

Due to pandemic and other numerous reasons, a child’s education could be interrupted. Having a proper education at childhood is very important. At early childhood, training and applying concepts can improve a child’s thinking and creative ability. In Sri Lanka, education for slow learners faces major challenges such as lack of parents’ knowledge on childhood education, less interaction with children and lack of education platforms to support slow learners. Thus, our team propose a mobile application which helps as a learning aid to improve early childhood education for slow learning children. The system is designed to teach fundamental skills at the early stage of these children.

Future Works

Slow Learners are children whose thinking skills and learn- ing performance have developed significantly more slower than the pace of his or her age. The kids Buddy mobile application is focused on slow learning children of age 4-6. The features included in this application are focused on improving the learning abilities of such slow learning children.There are four features namely Click and Find me for object recognition, Talking Dolly for Sinhala chatbot, Say it Right for improving Sinhala pronunciation and Math Champ for improving mathematical skills.

As for the future work, the mobile application will be further improved for the ios platform. All the features will be further developed. That is, in the Object recognizer the description will be generated in many other languages, in the Sinhala pronunciation trainer the skill levels will be increased. Further experiments will be carried out to expand the training examples in dataset. We also expect to conduct pilot testing of kids buddy mobile application to identify the necessary improvements needed for the next phase.

Results & Discussion

In the objects recognition feature, the Wikipedia API and the Google API is used to generate a Sinhala description about the identified objects. YOLOv4 model can identify objects successfully using bounding box with a high accuracy percentage in a given image.

In the communication trainer,classifier uses the model. Response is classified and produced by each sentence. Responses are aligned with intent definition. Before processing, it is verified that the tag meets the intent if one or more classes are higher than the threshold. Classification list can be treated as stack and check the alternatives to match with intents. Accuracy will be increased when the dataset and training is increased. The chatbot was designed as digital learning tool to facilitate educational supports for children.

In the Sinhala pronunciation trainer, by analysing the results it was clear that the by using the DTW algorithm it is able to measure the similarity of audio wave plots. Thus is is able to measure the pronunciation accuracy.

In the mathematics feature, it was revealed that with the help of the time based math quizes, the child is able to respond to a question quickily. Therefore, it clear that this component helps to improve a slow learners math and thinking skills.
